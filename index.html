Case Studies

Selected work

A few examples of how I’ve used measurement, audience strategy, and creative to move opinion, close partisan gaps, and drive behavior change.

Meta · Measurement
Building a policy marketing measurement roadmap
High‑stakes policy campaigns were being guided by weekly sentiment surveys with no clear view of which channels or partners actually drove change. I designed a multi‑layered measurement roadmap using brand lift, matched‑market tests, and a centralized learning agenda. Result: teams shifted budget into channels with proven incremental lift and started planning campaigns from evidence, not intuition.
Brand lift · Incremental lift · Learning agenda
Meta · Audience strategy
Reframing who is actually persuadable
Internal intuition said younger, tech‑savvy audiences were our natural allies on competition and safety issues. Working with an external agency, I championed a persuadable audience model that surfaced older adults and parents as the real leverage points. Result: campaigns using the model delivered 2–3 point gains in key metrics versus our broader, intuition‑driven targeting.
Persuadable modeling · Policy elites · Targeting
Meta · Bipartisan playbook
Closing partisan engagement gaps
Research showed our policy marketing was landing with Democrats but underperforming with Republican stakeholders in key districts. I led a cross‑functional push to map Republican‑leaning regions and media habits, then pivoted our strategy toward local TV news and locally grounded narratives. Result: we saw a 14 percent increase in message resonance among conservative audiences without losing ground with Democrats.
Bipartisan strategy · Local media · Narrative design
Intuit · Trust
From vanity metrics to trust engine
Public Affairs was optimized for impressions and CTRs with no line of sight to trust, credibility, or message pull‑through. I reoriented the program around competitive landscape analysis, a learning‑agenda culture, and KPIs tied to trust survey lift and cost per point of lift. Result: budget now follows persuasion impact, campaigns are more efficient, and teams make decisions faster with shared evidence.
Reputation · CPPL · Learning culture
UNICEF · UNGA
Owning the conversation on children
At UN General Assembly, UNICEF needed to stand out and keep the focus on children, not abstract goals. I built an onsite real‑time content studio and a distinct visual identity so we could turn small hallway moments into platform‑native stories in hours, not weeks. Result: UNICEF owned about 46 percent of the online conversation about children and SDGs that week and set the model for future high‑level events.
Real‑time content · Social · Share of voice
FEVE · Experiential
Making sustainability playful at checkout
Ocean plastic anxiety was rising, and glass packaging needed a way to feel like an everyday, ocean‑friendly choice at the shelf. I led a multi‑market experiential campaign where animated dolphins “thanked” shoppers at checkout, then amplified the reactions with social and rigorous brand‑lift studies. Result: we drove a 6.6‑point lift in glass purchase intent in Italy and a 5‑point lift in perceptions of glass as ocean‑friendly in Spain.
Experiential retail · Behavior change · Brand lift
